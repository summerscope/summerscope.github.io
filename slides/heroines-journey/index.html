what called you to tech?

refusal of the call

You might be tempted to let fear, insecurity, or a sense of inadequacy win. Especially in the beginning, it's easy to second-guess yourself, to question why you're trying to do this in the first place.
This phase is temporary. You only have to get your first gig once.
It took me over 6 months to get my first digital design gig, and I heard A LOT of nos during that time. It was tough, but it was worth it.

About CVs

or, why writing about yourself is THE WORST

"The conflation of the commercial and the human"
A gross but unacknowledged aspect of the job hunting process. We are expected to sell ourselves as if we were products on the shelf.
  • Sensitive!
  • Strong!
  • Confident!
  • Effective cleaning!
  • Maximum service!
Copy for a person or a roll of toilet paper!?

SHHHH!
A dirty little secret

the further you get, the less you need to write this sort of sales-pitch for yourself. Caveat* depending on which sort of gigs you apply for as you continue...

Leverage your non-tech experience

Your other life and working experiences DO matter in tech. They often help you think about problems differently, or help you scaffold ways to problem solve from other domains. Don't undersell your non-tech experience.
"If it helps you communicate, problem-solve, or human better, it's relevant."

4.
heroine's return

time to share the goods

Give talks before you feel ready to! Aim for roles you think are a bit above your skill set or experience! Say yes to opportunities which are exiting but scary! Look for ways to push yourself if you get too comfortable.

giving back

Support others who are trying to crack into the tech industry. Give them feedback on their CVs. Help point them to companies you think might be a good fit.

i can haz be a mentor!?

Mentoring doesn't have to be a formal arrangement or organised through a company. It often works great as a friendship with a little more experience on one side. Design it to suit both your needs.

trust your gut

rule of thumb about joining new companies - you have a couple of months to push your biggest, scariest new ideas as an agenda because after that initial period you go native as you assimilate. I think the same is true of larger tech industry as a whole - if you get too native it's hard to see what needs change.

embrace your super powers

(they're different for everyone)

mine: I'm silly! I giggle alot, and I'm very. VERY. easily amused. I'm now much more ok with showing this side of myself because it's a great coping mechanism in stressful situations. Life is filled with hard problems and complex shit and things that will make you grey.
Don't believe me? Believe Grey's Anatomy (plus tons of neuroscience and research and shit)

what's your super power?

(If no takers, ask) Who's great at spotting the difference between 2 pictures? (code) Who's great at reading between the lines to understand what someone's saying? (empathy / UX) Who's great at breaking a job down into a to-do list? (planning / estimation)
