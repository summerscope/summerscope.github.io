Capturing evidence

Using decibel scale log odds

Why decibels?

Our minds are thoroughly conditioned to the base 10 number system, and base 10 logarithms have an immediate, clear intuitive meaning to all of us.
— E.T. Jaynes, Probability Theory: The Logic of Science (4.2)
$$ P(H|D) = \frac{P(D|H) P(H)}{P(D)} $$
\[ \begin{align*} O(H|D) &= \frac{P(H|D)}{P(\neg H|D)} \\ &= O(H) \frac{P(D|H) \cancel{P(D)}}{P(D|\neg H) \cancel{P(D)}} \end{align*} \]
$$ e(H|D) = 10 \log_{10}O(H|D) $$
$$ e(H|D) = \underbrace{\vphantom{\sum_i \left[\frac{P}{P}\right]} e(H)}_{\text{Prior}} + \underbrace{\sum_i 10\,\log_{10} \left[\frac{P(D_i|H)}{P(D_i|\neg H)}\right]}_{\text{Data Likelihood Ratios}} $$
