What your personal brand says about you

Applying for gigs is like trying to entice an attractive human. If you're in demand, everyone wants you; employers can smell the funk of desperation a mile away.

first date

got an interview?

tips for success

do your prep

Learn as much about the company as you can beforehand

Who are their clients? What problems / opportunities are they facing?

Have some concrete ideas as to why you're a good skill set and culture fit for them

Do a pre-mortem: think of all the reasons you won't get the job and pre-empt them with a solution.

practice

Prepare for & practise answering questions you know you’re going to get asked:

What's your understanding of the role?

What relevant experience do you have that will help in the role?

For juniors, consider work, uni or experience with personal projects that might demonstrate your problem solving capabilities

fit the outfit

Dress to match the people you wanna work with

Don't overdress (looks too keen or stiff)

Don't underdress (looks like you don't care)

be on time

Seriously, be on time. Not late, and no more than 5 minutes early

turn the tables

Treat a job interview as though you are interviewing the company

Prepare some questions to ask

Be interested in them

star methodology

Situation

Task

Action

Result

read more

break it down

Show your thinking, not just the end result

You're in the room to show your personality, potential and ability to learn

after the interview

Do a post-mortem: work out what you could do better, or which questions threw you off

Then work out a strategy to smash them next time

conversion

getting serious

Just like finding a romantic partner, not only do you need to be ready, open and excited to start the relationship - the business also needs the right set of circumstances to hire you: the right mentors on the team, the budget and time, etc. Keep in mind that employers are people too, and can be capricious. They may swipe right initially but change their mind before you've even walked into the office. You can't take it too personally, and you need to remember that what you bring to the table is only one part of the equation for whether or not they make you an offer.

in other words...

no, it's not all about you
