To understand what we mean by reproducible we get to the heart of what's been causing a bunch of ruffled feathers in academia these past few years...
What is the reproducibility crisis?
This refers to a movement challenging the status quo in science and specifically asks whether current incentives to publish results are resulting in poor research design and supurious research results.
You'll never believe what happened when scientists attempted to replicate 100 experiments...

Just wait til you see their results

Psychologists tried to recreate the experiments conducted by 100 studies, all published recently in academic journals.
"97% of the original results showed a statistically significant effect, this was reproduced in only 36% of the replication attempts"
https://digest.bps.org.uk/
What's to blame? Clickbait!
Seriously though, the incentives for publishing focus on new, unique findings. If those findings can turn into misleading clickbait so much the better!
